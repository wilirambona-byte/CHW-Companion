The Challenge

Why Community Health Workers are struggling

In many low-resource settings, Community Health Workers form the backbone of primary healthcare delivery. Yet they often work with limited guidance, fragmented tools and very little real-time support when they need it most.

Isolation in the field

CHWs often conduct household visits and community outreach alone, with no easy way to reach a supervisor or clinical peer when they face a difficult case.

Inconsistent training retention

Training quality and knowledge retention vary widely. Without refresher resources at their fingertips, the gap between training and daily practice grows over time.

Paper-based recording

Many CHWs still rely on paper registers and forms, making data collection slow, error-prone and difficult to act on at the facility or district level.

Weak supervision links

Supervision visits are infrequent and supervisors often lack the tools to track performance, identify gaps and deliver timely, constructive feedback.

Low motivation and retention

Without recognition, feedback or career pathways, many CHWs disengage. High turnover erodes community trust and disrupts continuity of care.

Fragmented health system data

CHW data rarely feeds back into district health systems in a timely way, limiting the ability of health planners to respond to emerging needs.

The Solution

A companion built for the work CHWs actually do

CHW Companion is a digital tool designed to sit alongside Community Health Workers throughout their daily work. It provides decision support, structured data collection, supervision linkages and learning resources, all in one place.

🩺

Clinical Decision Support

Step-by-step guidance for screening, referral and case management, adapted to the CHW service package and local protocols.

📋

Integrated Data Collection

Digital forms replace paper registers. Data is structured, timestamped and ready for review by supervisors and facility teams.

📡

Offline-First Design

The tool works without an internet connection, syncing automatically when connectivity returns. No CHW is left behind due to poor network coverage.

🤝

Supervision and Feedback

Supervisors can track visits, review submitted data and send feedback directly through the platform, reducing the burden of in-person check-ins.

📚

Learning and Refreshers

Short, visual learning modules help CHWs revisit key skills between formal training sessions, building confidence and competence over time.

Core Modules

  • Household registration and follow-up
  • Maternal and newborn health screening
  • Child health and nutrition monitoring
  • Malaria, TB and chronic disease case finding
  • Referral tracking and feedback loops
  • Stock and supply visibility
  • CHW performance dashboard
  • Supervisor review portal
  • In-app learning library
  • Community health mapping

Who It Serves

Designed for the people closest to communities

Every design decision in CHW Companion is shaped by the realities of working in rural and peri-urban settings with limited infrastructure and high community need.

👩‍⚕️

Community Health Workers

Frontline workers conducting home visits, health promotion and basic case management within their communities.

👨‍💼

CHW Supervisors

Facility-based nurses and health officers responsible for overseeing and supporting a cohort of CHWs.

🏥

Health Facilities

Primary health centres and district hospitals that receive referrals from CHWs and need visibility into community-level data.

📊

District Health Managers

Planners using data to allocate resources, monitor trends and improve performance across their catchment area.

About the Author

The person behind the concept

Wilfried Irambona
Medical Doctor and Public Health Professional, Burundi

Wilfried is a Medical Doctor and public health professional from Burundi, committed to strengthening primary healthcare systems and advancing equitable access to services for underserved communities, particularly in rural areas.

He has led integrated community health programmes and supported the implementation of digital health solutions for Community Health Workers with international organisations. He also contributed to national health policy processes, including the development of the Burundian Community Health Strategy 2025 to 2029, with a focus on digitalisation, CHW profiles, service packages and financing mechanisms.

Wilfried has a strong interest in data systems and AI-enabled tools to strengthen healthcare delivery and improve health system performance in resource-constrained settings.
